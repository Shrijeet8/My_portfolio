From JEE failure to building my engineering journey

Hi, I’m a Mechanical Engineering student at JSPM Rajarshi Shahu College of Engineering, Tathawade. Like many students, I once dreamed of cracking JEE and getting into an IIT. I gave it everything I had — late-night study sessions, coaching classes, mock tests — but when the results came, I didn’t get the rank I hoped for.

My lowest point

It was hard. I felt lost, like all the effort went to waste. I questioned myself — am I even capable? Did I just waste 2 years? But slowly, I realized something important: a single exam does not define my future. What matters is what I do next.

Choosing new path

I joined JSPM RSCOE to pursue Mechanical Engineering. At first, I carried that JEE disappointment with me. But I decided I would give my best here and rebuild myself. I got involved in the Formula Student Team - Redline Racing, where I work in the Structures domain, designing components using CATIA V5, AutoCAD, and analyzing in ANSYS.

Discovering web development

Alongside mechanical work, I began exploring something new — Web Development. I started learning HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. It was confusing at first, but slowly I began to love it. Now, I can build landing pages, blogs, and even my own portfolio!

How far I have come

  • Completed projects in both Mechanical and Web fields
  • Created my portfolio website using HTML & CSS
  • Learned CAD tools and started CFD & FEA analysis
  • Scored a CGPA above 8 in 1st year
  • Interned at Harihar Industries for CNC and VMC exposure
My message to students like me

If you didn’t crack JEE — it’s okay. That exam doesn’t decide your worth. I didn’t get into an IIT, but now I’m building skills that IITians would admire. It’s not where you start — it’s where you choose to go.

Every single day, I learn something new. And I know that by the time I graduate, I’ll be skilled, confident, and ready to create value — in tech, design, or even business.

The Journey Is Mine Now. And I’m Owning It.
